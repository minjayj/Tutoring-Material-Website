Tutor's Study Guide: Unit 1 Limits & Continuity

The 3-Part Definition of Continuity at a Point $x = c$:

  1. $f(c)$ is defined.
  2. $\lim_{x \to c} f(x)$ exists (left-hand limit equals right-hand limit).
  3. $\lim_{x \to c} f(x) = f(c)$.

Evaluating Limits Strategy: Always try direct substitution first. If you yield an indeterminate form like $\frac{0}{0}$ or $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$, use algebraic manipulation (factoring, multiplying by the conjugate) or apply L'Hôpital's Rule: $\lim_{x \to c} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to c} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$.

Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT): If $f$ is continuous on the closed interval $[a, b]$, and $k$ is any number between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, then there is at least one number $c$ in the interval such that $f(c) = k$.
